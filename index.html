Exchange Rate Analysis: USD to INR

Unraveling India's Currency Exchange Patterns with Data

1. The Curiosity Behind Exchange Rate Fluctuations

As someone passionate about data, I frequently analyze patterns in economic indicators. Exchange rates play a crucial role in global trade, investment, and economic stability, but how have they evolved over time? Examining historical shifts in the USD to INR exchange rate can provide valuable insights into India’s economic development, inflation trends, and policy decisions. To explore this, I conducted an analysis of the yearly exchange rate of INR against the USD from 1947 to 2024 using historical data.

2. Gathering the Historical Exchange Rate Data

To perform this analysis, I needed accurate and comprehensive data. Here’s how I collected the exchange rate data:

📌 Sources Used:

  • (Reserve Bank of India) historical reports

    5. Observations

    How Has the Exchange Rate Changed Over Time?

    To get a clear understanding, I divided the exchange rate timeline into key economic phases:

    📌 1947 - 1970: Fixed Exchange Rate Era

  • The INR was pegged to the British Pound until 1966 and later to the USD.
  • Exchange rate remained relatively stable (~₹4.8 per USD).

    • 📌 1970 - 1991: Gradual Devaluation

  • INR depreciated gradually as India transitioned from a controlled economy.
  • By 1991, the exchange rate hit ₹17.90 per USD, leading to economic reforms.

    • 📌 1991 - 2000: Liberalization & Market-Driven Exchange Rate

  • moved to a market-determined exchange rate post Economic Reforms.
  • Sharp depreciation, reaching ₹45 per USD by 2000.

    • 📌 2000 - 2024: Volatility & Globalization

  • The INR fluctuated due to global financial crises, inflation, and trade policies.
  • By 2024, the exchange rate hovered around ₹83.5 per USD.

    • 6. Analyzing the Factors Influencing Exchange Rate

    Once I understood the data patterns, I explored statistical and predictive models.

    Key Factors Influencing Exchange Rates

    📈 Inflation Rate – Higher inflation weakens the INR.

    📊 Interest Rates – Higher interest rates attract foreign investments, strengthening the INR.

    🌎 Trade Balance – Trade deficits lead to depreciation.

    💰 Foreign Reserves & FDI – Higher reserves stabilize the INR.

    Statistical & Predictive Modeling Approaches

    🔹 Time Series Analysis (ARIMA Model) – To predict future exchange rate trends.

    🔹 Linear Regression – To correlate economic indicators with exchange rate movement.

    7. Key Observations & Takeaways

    🔹 Exchange rates have steadily depreciated over time, influenced by economic policies and global factors.

    🔹 Major devaluations occurred during economic crises (1991, 2008).

    🔹 Post-1991 reforms, market forces played a dominant role in INR valuation.

    🔹 High inflation and trade deficits have consistently contributed to INR depreciation.

    📌 Final Thoughts: Understanding these trends helps in making informed financial decisions, investment strategies, and economic policy assessments.
